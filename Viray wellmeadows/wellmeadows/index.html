Seneca.

Wherever the art of Medicine is loved,

there is also a love of Humanity

Medicine is a science of uncertainty and an art of probability.

To study the phenomena of disease without books

is to sail an uncharted sea,

while to study books without patients

is not to go to sea at all.

The aim of medicine is to prevent disease and prolong life;

the ideal of medicine is to eliminate the need of a physician.

In nothing do men more nearly approach

the gods than in giving health to men.

The art of healing comes from nature, not from the physician.

Therefore the physician must start from nature, with an open mind.

Cure sometimes, treat often, comfort always.

A doctor can bury his mistakes, but an architect can only advise his clients to plant vines.

Medicine sometimes snatches away health, sometimes gives it.

People pay the doctor for his trouble; for his kindness they still remain in his debt.

Your mental health is a priority. Your happiness is an essential. Your self-care is a necessity.
